## TL;DR (lead with this)
- Reproduced the core claim of **PRAGMA** (Revolut's financial foundation model): a small
  Transformer pretrained with **masked modeling on transaction sequences**, then **frozen +
  linear-probed**, **beats a strong LightGBM incumbent** on fraud — **PR-AUC 0.49 vs 0.043**
  under a leakage-free as-of-date eval.
- All on a laptop (M4 Max), open data (IBM TabFormer), ~3–14M params.
- Three quantitative studies: **model-size scaling**, **context-length scaling**, and a
  **time-since-last-event (Δt) embedding** — plus the honest caveats (seed noise, a negative
  retrieval result).

## 1. Motivation & background
- The foundation-model recipe (pretrain once, adapt cheaply) has swept NLP/vision — does it
  work for **tabular banking event streams**? Cite PRAGMA (arXiv:2604.08649); the FFM idea.
- The incumbent in fraud/credit is **gradient-boosted trees on hand-engineered features**.
  Question: can a self-supervised sequence model beat that, and what actually drives it?
- What this post is: a *scaled, honest* reproduction — including what didn't work.

## 2. Data: sourcing & split
- **IBM TabFormer** synthetic credit-card transactions: 24.4M txns, 2000 users, 6139
  (user,card) sequences, **0.12% fraud**. Apache-2.0, no auth. (Note: PRAGMA's own 207B-token
  corpus is proprietary — hence the open substitute.)
- Schema: user/card, timestamp, amount, use-chip, merchant/MCC/city/state/zip, errors, `Is Fraud?`.
- **Split = per-(user,card) sequence** (whole users held out) — tests generalisation to unseen
  users. *Sidebar:* the temporal split is shift-confounded here (fraud rate swings by year;
  LightGBM 0.94→0.79 ROC), so we headline the seq split.
- **The leakage subtlety worth a paragraph:** PRAGMA is bidirectional, so naively an event's
  embedding can see *future* events in its window. The paper avoids this by cutting history at
  an "evaluation point"; we replicate with **as-of-date scoring** (causal mask). Show it costs
  little (0.24→0.22) — the win is real, not future-peeking.

## 3. Model design (mini-PRAGMA)
- **Key–value–time tokenization**: each event → set of (field,value) tokens; numeric =
  percentile buckets, categoricals = vocab, high-cardinality = hashing, calendar = hour/dow.
- **Two-stage encoder**: Event Encoder (set-transformer over a txn's fields → `[EVT]`) →
  **History Encoder** (transformer over the event sequence, **RoPE keyed on event time**).
- **Objective**: masked modeling (reconstruct masked field-values), BERT-style mixed masking.
- **Adaptation**: freeze backbone → linear probe on record embeddings (the purest "are the
  representations good?" test); as-of-date scoring for fraud.
- Sizes: nano 2.9M / mini 7.5M / small 13.7M. Trained on MPS; note the NaN-mask fix + non-finite-grad guard as an "engineering reality" aside.

## 4. Baseline & metrics
- **LightGBM** on causal engineered features (rolling amount stats, time-since-last, online flag,
  categoricals) — the honest incumbent. **PR-AUC 0.043 / ROC 0.94.**
- Why **PR-AUC** is the headline (0.12% base rate → ROC-AUC is misleading; PR-AUC and
  Recall@Precision are what matter operationally).

## 5. Headline result
- Frozen mini-PRAGMA + linear probe **>> LightGBM**: best (small@6000) **PR-AUC 0.495,
  Recall@P0.5 0.52** (catches >½ of fraud at 50% precision) vs LightGBM's 0.043 / 0.00.
- The paper's central claim reproduces at laptop scale.

## 6. Ablations & scaling  ← the meat, one subsection + figure each

### 6a. Scaling with model size  →  `figures/fig1_model_scaling.png`
- nano→mini→small; capacity clearly helps.
- **The twist:** at a fixed 3000-step budget small *dipped* (undertrained); doubling steps →
  0.495. Lesson: bigger models need proportionally more steps; step-matched sweeps understate them.

### 6b. Scaling with sequence length  →  `figures/fig2_seq_length.png`
- Steep gains to ~L=32, then **keeps climbing (diminishing) through L=256 — not yet plateaued**.
- ROC saturates early (~L=8); the marginal gains are at high precision.
- Caveat: nano is capacity-limited so likely understates the context benefit.

### 6c. The Δt (time-since-last) embedding  →  `figures/fig3_delta_t.png`
- Motivation: RoPE puts time in *attention*; a log-bucketed Δt puts recency in *content*.
- Single-seed it looked like a clean +8% win — **but with 3 seeds it's within noise** (this is
  where the honesty pays off narratively).

### 6d. Numeric encoding (bucket vs PLE vs periodic) — short
- Surprising: **hard quantile bucketing beat both** continuous encodings (PLE/periodic), likely
  because the MLM target is bucket-shaped. Counter to the tabular-DL literature — good hook.

### 6e. RoPE ablation (time vs index vs none) — short
- Means order as predicted (time > index > none) but seed noise dominates at n=3 — directional only.

## 7. The methodology lessons (a strong section — readers love this)
- **Leakage is subtle:** an out-of-time split doesn't fix within-window future-peeking; task
  framing (as-of-date) does.
- **Seed noise ≥ effect:** nano@3000 swings ±0.05 PR-AUC across seeds — as large as several of
  our single-seed deltas. Multi-seed or it didn't happen.
- **Pre-flights save days:** a 3-second data check killed a planned retrieval feature (see 8).

## 8. What didn't work / limitations
- **SIM-style retrieval — killed by a pre-flight.** We checked whether fraud has long-range
  structure: a card's distant fraud history has **no** predictive lift (0.15×), so retrieving
  old events can't help *this* task. Fraud here is a short-range burst process. (Real banking
  data likely differs.)
- Synthetic data; single downstream task; capacity-limited nano for ablations; single-seed
  headline numbers for mini/small.

## 9. Takeaways
- The FFM recipe genuinely works for transaction data, even tiny + frozen.
- The wins that *survive scrutiny*: beating GBDT, model-size scaling, context helps to ≥256.
- The wins that *don't yet*: Δt, RoPE-flavour — need more seeds.
- Honest science > a clean-looking plot.
